## Forks and Redistributions

GPLv3 gives you the right to fork and redistribute the code.  When you do so, the following
trademark rules apply:

### Allowed

- Distributing modified or unmodified source code under GPLv3, provided you comply with that
  license's conditions.
- Stating factually that your fork is "based on Jitterbugs" or "derived from the Jitterbugs
  project" in documentation, changelogs, or similar notices.
- Linking back to this upstream repository.

### Not Allowed

- Using "Jitterbug", "Jitterbugs", or confusingly similar names as the name of your fork,
  product, or service without prior written permission.
- Using the Jitterbugs logos or trade dress in a way that implies your fork is an official
  release or is endorsed by the Jitterbugs project owners.
- Presenting your fork in a manner that could mislead users into believing they are using the
  official Jitterbugs software or hardware.

## Requesting Permission

To request a trademark license (e.g., for a compatible product or distribution partnership),
open an issue in this repository or contact the project maintainers directly.
